**Unlock the Power of Hydrogen Peroxide: 15 Cleaning Uses You Never Knew Existed**

When it comes to cleaning products, many of us reach for the same old staples like bleach and ammonia. However, hydrogen peroxide is often overlooked as a versatile and eco-friendly alternative. In reality, hydrogen peroxide has been used for decades in various industries, from medicine to manufacturing. In this article, we'll delve into the surprising world of hydrogen peroxide cleaning uses and explore 15 ways to repurpose this powerful oxidizing agent.

### 1. Natural Disinfectant
Hydrogen peroxide is a natural disinfectant that's effective against a wide range of bacteria, viruses, and fungi. Mix equal parts water and hydrogen peroxide in a spray bottle for an all-purpose cleaner. Spray on surfaces, let it sit for 10-15 minutes, and then wipe clean with a microfiber cloth.

### 2. Whiter Whites
Soak your white clothes in a solution of equal parts hydrogen peroxide and water to remove tough stains and brighten whites. Let it sit overnight before washing as usual.

### 3. Remove Scratches from Polished Wood
For minor scratches on polished wood surfaces, apply a small amount of hydrogen peroxide directly to the affected area using a cotton swab. Gently rub the solution in with a soft cloth, then buff with a clean cloth to remove any excess.

### 4. Natural Bleach Alternative for Laundry
When you need a strong bleach alternative for laundry, use hydrogen peroxide instead. Mix 1 cup of hydrogen peroxide with 2 cups of water as a substitute for chlorine bleach.

### 5. Remove Stubborn Stains from Carpets
For tough stains on carpets, mix equal parts hydrogen peroxide and dish soap to create a solution. Apply the solution directly to the stain, let it sit for 10-15 minutes, and then blot with a clean cloth.

### 6. Disinfect Baby Bottles and Pacifiers
To keep baby bottles and pacifiers clean and sanitized, mix equal parts water and hydrogen peroxide in a bottle. Soak each item for 5-10 minutes before rinsing thoroughly.

### 7. Clean Grout Lines
For grout lines, apply a mixture of baking soda and hydrogen peroxide to create a paste. Scrub the area with an old toothbrush for 5-10 minutes before rinsing with warm water.

### 8. Deodorize Refrigerator Coils
To eliminate unpleasant odors from refrigerator coils, mix equal parts water and hydrogen peroxide in a spray bottle. Spray directly onto the coils and wipe clean with a soft cloth.

### 9. Remove Mold and Mildew from Walls
For mold and mildew growth on walls, apply a solution of equal parts water and hydrogen peroxide to an old toothbrush. Scrub the affected area for 5-10 minutes before rinsing thoroughly.

### 10. Disinfect Keyboards and Mice
To keep electronic devices clean and sanitized, mix equal parts water and hydrogen peroxide in a spray bottle. Spray directly onto keyboards and mice, then wipe clean with a soft cloth.

### 11. Clean Makeup Brushes
For makeup brushes, mix equal parts water and hydrogen peroxide to create a solution. Soak the brushes for 10-15 minutes before rinsing thoroughly.

### 12. Remove Soap Scum from Showers
To remove soap scum from shower walls, apply a mixture of baking soda and hydrogen peroxide to create a paste. Scrub the area with an old toothbrush for 5-10 minutes before rinsing with warm water.

### 13. Disinfect Pet Bedding
For pet bedding, mix equal parts water and hydrogen peroxide in a spray bottle. Spray directly onto the bedding and let it air dry to eliminate any bacteria or odors.

### 14. Clean Jewelry Cleaning Cloths
To keep jewelry cleaning cloths clean and sanitized, mix equal parts water and hydrogen peroxide in a solution. Soak the cloth for 10-15 minutes before rinsing thoroughly.

### 15. Remove Ink Stains from Clothing
For ink stains on clothing, apply a small amount of hydrogen peroxide directly to the affected area using a cotton swab. Gently rub the solution in with a soft cloth and let it air dry before washing as usual.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### **Q: Is hydrogen peroxide safe for use around children and pets?**
A: **Yes**, hydrogen peroxide is generally considered safe when used in moderation. However, always dilute the solution to 3% concentration or less and keep out of reach of children and pets.

### **Q: Can I use hydrogen peroxide as a substitute for chlorine bleach?**
A: **No**, while hydrogen peroxide can be used as a natural bleach alternative, it's not a direct replacement. Hydrogen peroxide is typically less effective at removing tough stains and whiter whites.

### **Q: How long does hydrogen peroxide take to work?**
A: **The effectiveness of hydrogen peroxide depends on the surface being cleaned**, but generally, 10-15 minutes is sufficient for most applications. Always follow the recommended dilution ratio and usage instructions for best results.
