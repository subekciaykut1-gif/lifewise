**The Ultimate Fix for a Toilet That Runs Constantly: A Comprehensive Guide**

Are you tired of dealing with a toilet that constantly runs, wasting water and increasing your utility bills? You're not alone. According to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), a single toilet can waste up to 200 gallons of water per day. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fix a toilet that runs constantly, saving you money and reducing your environmental impact.

**Why Does My Toilet Run Constantly?**

Before we dive into the solution, it's essential to understand why your toilet is running in the first place. There are several common reasons for this issue:

*   **Faulty Flapper**: The flapper is responsible for sealing the connection between the tank and bowl. If it's not functioning correctly, water will continue to flow.
*   **Clogged Overflow Tube**: The overflow tube helps regulate the water level in the tank. If it's clogged, water can spill over into the bowl, causing it to run constantly.
*   **Imbalanced Fill Valve**: A misaligned or faulty fill valve can cause the toilet to run excessively.

**Fixing a Toilet That Runs Constantly: A Step-by-Step Guide**

To fix your toilet, follow these steps:

1.  **Turn Off the Water Supply**: Locate the shut-off valve behind the toilet and turn it clockwise to shut off the water supply.
2.  **Check and Clean the Overflow Tube**: Use a plunger or plumbing snake to clear any blockages in the overflow tube.
3.  **Adjust the Fill Valve**: Adjust the fill valve to ensure it's aligned properly and not too high, which can cause the toilet to run constantly.

    •   Check the water level in the tank: It should be about halfway between the bottom of the overflow pipe and the top of the tank.
    •   Adjust the float arm: If the float arm is not in its correct position, it may prevent the fill valve from shutting off properly.
4.  **Replace the Flapper**: If your toilet has a flapper, replace it with a new one to ensure proper sealing.
5.  **Test the Toilet**: Turn the water supply back on and flush the toilet several times to test if the issue is fixed.

**Additional Tips for Fixing a Running Toilet**

*   **Use a Water-Efficient Toilet**: Consider installing a low-flow toilet, which uses significantly less water per flush than traditional toilets.
*   **Regular Maintenance**: Regularly check and maintain your toilet's components to prevent future issues.
*   **Consider Professional Help**: If you're not comfortable with DIY repairs or if the issue persists after following these steps, consider hiring a professional plumber.
