**Couple Builds Shipping Container Home for $35,000 — Banks Called Them Crazy**

Meet Sarah and Mike, a young couple who dared to dream big – literally. They wanted to build their own home from scratch, but not just any home. They had a vision of a sustainable, eco-friendly abode that would be both affordable and unique. After months of research and planning, they finally found the perfect solution: a shipping container home.

The couple purchased two 20-foot shipping containers for $6,000 each, which brought their total cost to just under $12,000. They then hired a local contractor to help them build out the containers into a cozy three-bedroom home. The result was a stunning, modern space that blended seamlessly into its natural surroundings.

But what really sets this project apart is how the couple managed to stay within budget – an impressive feat considering the cost of shipping and building a custom home can easily exceed $100,000. So, what were their secrets? Let's dive in and find out.

### **Design and Planning**

The first step was designing and planning their dream home. Sarah and Mike spent countless hours researching different styles and layouts until they landed on a design that worked for them. They chose a simple, open-plan layout with large windows to maximize natural light and take advantage of the stunning views.

*Here are some key design elements that made their shipping container home stand out:*

1. **Reclaimed wood accents**
2. **Eco-friendly materials**
3. **Innovative storage solutions**

These thoughtful touches not only added character to the space but also helped keep costs down.

### **Budget Breakdown**

So, how did Sarah and Mike manage to stay within budget? Here's a breakdown of their expenses:

* Shipping containers: $12,000
* Building materials: $8,000
* Labor costs: $10,000
* Permits and inspections: $2,000
* Miscellaneous (furniture, appliances, etc.): $3,000

Total cost: $35,000

### **Tips for Building a Shipping Container Home on a Budget**

While Sarah and Mike's experience was unique, there are some key takeaways that can be applied to any budget-building project:

1. **Research, research, research**: Spend time learning about different materials, designs, and construction methods.
2. **Be flexible**: Be willing to compromise on certain elements to stay within budget.
3. **Plan ahead**: Allow plenty of time for design and planning to ensure everything fits together smoothly.
