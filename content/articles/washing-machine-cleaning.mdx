**The Shocking Truth About Washing Machine Cleaning: Why You're Probably Doing It Wrong**

A dirty washing machine can be a breeding ground for mold, mildew, and bacteria, making your clothes less clean and potentially even harming your health. In fact, according to the American Cleaning Institute, 1 in 5 washing machines contains more bacteria than a toilet handle! Don't let this happen to you – it's time to learn how to properly clean your washing machine.

**What You Need to Know**

Before we dive into the nitty-gritty of washing machine cleaning, here are some key things to keep in mind:

*   Most modern washing machines have a self-cleaning cycle or a "clean washer" setting. However, these cycles may not be as effective as you think.
*   Regular cleaning can help prevent odors and reduce the risk of mold growth.
*   Cleaning your washing machine regularly can also improve its performance and extend its lifespan.

**The Best Way to Clean Your Washing Machine**

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to properly clean your washing machine:

1.  **Leave the lid open after each use**: This allows moisture to escape, reducing the risk of mold growth.
2.  **Run a cleaning cycle**: Check your user manual for a self-cleaning cycle or "clean washer" setting. If you don't have this feature, run an empty hot water cycle with a cup of white vinegar.
3.  **Wipe down the exterior**: Use a damp cloth to wipe down the exterior of the machine, paying attention to any areas with visible buildup or stains.

**Deep Cleaning Tips**

For tougher stains and odors, try these deep cleaning tips:

*   **Baking soda and hot water**: Make a paste by mixing baking soda and hot water. Apply it to tough stains and let it sit for 30 minutes before rinsing.
*   **Vinegar and steam**: Run an empty cycle with vinegar and steam to help loosen any built-up debris.

**Frequently Asked Questions**

### How often should I clean my washing machine?

It's recommended to clean your washing machine every 1-2 months, depending on usage. If you notice a buildup of dirt or odors, it's time for a deep clean!

### Can I use bleach to clean my washing machine?

While bleach can be effective at killing bacteria and mildew, it's not the best option for cleaning your washing machine. Bleach can damage certain materials and leave harsh chemical residues.

### How do I prevent mold growth in my washing machine?

To prevent mold growth, ensure good airflow around the machine, clean regularly, and check for any signs of moisture buildup.
