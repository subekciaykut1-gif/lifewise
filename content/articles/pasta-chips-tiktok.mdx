## The Pasta Chips Recipe That Took Over TikTok — We Tested It So You Don't Have To

If you're anything like us, your social media feeds have been filled with videos of crispy, golden pasta chips, begging to be devoured in one sitting. **The #pastachipschallenge** has taken over TikTok, and we can't blame you for wanting to give it a try – after all, who wouldn't want a healthier alternative to traditional potato chips? In this article, we'll dive into the recipe that started it all and provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to make these addictive pasta chips at home.

### Ingredients You Need

Before we begin, let's take a look at what you'll need to get started:

- 1 pound of **pasta shapes** (we recommend using penne or fusilli)
- 2 tablespoons of olive oil
- Salt, to taste
- Optional: Additional seasonings such as garlic powder, paprika, or chili flakes

### Step-by-Step Guide to Making Pasta Chips

Now that we have our ingredients, let's get started with the cooking process:

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. Rinse your pasta shapes under cold water and pat them dry using paper towels or a clean kitchen towel.
3. In a large bowl, drizzle olive oil over your dried pasta shapes and sprinkle with salt. If desired, add additional seasonings to taste.
4. Toss the pasta shapes until they're evenly coated in oil and seasoning.

### **The Crisping Process**

Now that our pasta is prepared, we need to crisp it up:

- Spread the pasta out in a single layer on your prepared baking sheet. You may need to do this in batches depending on the size of your sheet.
- Bake for 10-12 minutes or until crispy and golden brown, flipping them halfway through.

### **Tips and Variations**

* To get that perfect crunch, make sure not to overcrowd your baking sheet.
* For an extra crispy texture, try baking at a higher temperature (425°F/220°C) for a shorter amount of time (8-10 minutes).

### Frequently Asked Questions

#### What type of pasta works best for making pasta chips?

We recommend using **penne** or **fusilli**, as these shapes hold their shape well and provide a nice surface area for crisping.

#### How do I prevent my pasta from becoming too soggy when baking?

To combat sogginess, make sure your pasta is completely dry before baking. If you notice that some parts of the pasta are still damp, give them a few extra minutes in the oven to crisp up.

#### Can I use different seasonings on my pasta chips?

Absolutely! Experiment with various seasoning combinations to find your favorite flavor. Some options include **garlic powder**, **paprika**, or **chili flakes**.
